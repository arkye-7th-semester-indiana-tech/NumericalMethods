\section{Discovering Multiplicity}

	If $x = \alpha$ is root of the function. Do continuous derivatives until:
	\begin{align}
		f(a) &=  0& \notag \\
		f'(a) &= 0& \notag \\
		f^{(n-1)}(a) &= 0& \notag \\
		f^{(n)}(a) &\neq 0& \\
		\label{eq:multiplicity}
	\end{align}

	The function in root $x = \alpha$ have multiplicity $n$.
